Business Context

"Investigate how the client can improve retention of its high performing employees and predict exactly which employees are most at risk of leaving."

Losing top talent is a massive cost to any organisation. The objective of this engagement was to use historical HR data (15,000 employees) to understand why high performers leave and build an early-warning system to prevent it.

"On average, the turnover rate for high performers was 7 percentage points higher than the overall average."

Key Findings & Insights

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) revealed significant departmental disparities: HR and Accounting had the highest turnover (>31%), while R&D showed strong retention.

  • Satisfaction & Workload: High-performing leavers experienced 40% less satisfaction than their peers who stayed. They were carrying 1.4x more projects and had spent 35% more time in the company.
  • Overtime Burnout: Top talent that left were working 26% more monthly hours.
  • Lack of Recognition: Upward mobility was stagnant. Nearly 96% of the retained top performers had not received a promotion in the last 5 years.

Predictive Modelling

Due to the non-linear nature of employee behaviour, linear models like Logistic Regression underperformed. A suite of algorithms was tested using RepeatedStratifiedKFold cross-validation.

XGBoost emerged as the clear winner, achieving over 97% accuracy in identifying flight risks.

Influential Variables

The model successfully flagged 22 employees with over 70% probability to leave in the near future. Critical intervention was recommended for the 10 identified high-performers.

Strategic Recommendations

Based on the data, the client should shift from reactive hiring to proactive talent management:

  • Reward Heavy Lifters: Introduce bonuses, extra PTO, or formal recognitions specifically targeting high performers working overtime or leading multiple projects.
  • Performance-Based Compensation: Conduct periodic, data-driven salary reviews. The data showed medium/low salary bands were highly susceptible to attrition.
  • Cross-Departmental Learning: Investigate and replicate the successful retention strategies utilised by the R&D department across HR and Accounting.
